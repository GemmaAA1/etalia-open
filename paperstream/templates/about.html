The Story

PubStream was started by two researchers at University of California, San Francisco in 2015, swamped by the overflow of scientific articles published. With a little knowledge of web development and natural language processing and some amount of pugnacity, they started to elaborate a tool that will ease the research community keeping up with the tremendous number of scientific articles published each month from an ever increasing variety of sources. This tool came to life as a web application. We called it PubStream.

Why PubStream?

Each month, about 100,000 scientific articles are published in thousands of different journals, conferences and websites. Keeping up with this fast evolving knowledge is a daunting task and little has been done to facilitate it. Searching by keywords on search engine like PubMed or Google scholar leads to partial findings. Email alerts focused on topics or authors are difficult to manage and become rapidly outdated. Weekly digest emails are often noisy. And manually browsing journal website and blogs searching for recent publications is a time-consuming process.

Our Vision

With PubStream, we intend to facilitate this search process, saving researchers time and making sure they are not missing any important findings. By leveraging the content of reference software library with recent advances in natural language processing and machine learning, PubStream builds a scientific news stream that match your personalized interests.
