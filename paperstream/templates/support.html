Frequently Asked Questions

How do PubStream works?

PubStream checks what you are reading in your Reference Library. PubStream leverages the content of your library using recent advances in natural language processing, machine learning, and statistics to understand your interests. PubStream builds news streams that match your personal interests based on your library text content. Reading a new paper or extending your research interests? PubStream will automatically adapt.

How can I use PubStream?

To use PubStream you need access to the internet and an account with a reference library (e.g. Mendelay or Zotero). Use your reference library credentials to sign in to PubStream and start browsing your personal stream of recommended publications.

What makes PubStream and Google Scholar?

PubStream recommends publications based on the content of your reference library unlike Google Scholar, which makes suggestions based on your publication and citation records only.

How often are recommendations updated?

PubStream updates recommendations daily. You can also subscribe to receive weekly notifications via email.

Which publications sources are fetched by PubStream?

Currently, PubStream fetches publications available on MedLine, the Arxiv.org and articles published in Elsevier journals.
