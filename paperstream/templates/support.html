Frequently Asked Questions

How does PubStream work?

PubStream checks what you are reading in your Reference Library. PubStream leverages the content of your library using recent advances in natural language processing, machine learning, and statistics to understand your interests. PubStream builds news streams that match your personal interests based on your library text content. Reading a new paper or extending your research interests? PubStream will automatically adapt.

How can I use PubStream?

To use PubStream you need access to the internet and an account with a reference library (e.g. Zotero or Mendeley). Use your reference library credentials to sign in to PubStream and start browsing your personal stream of recommended publications.

Why PubStream only support Zotero and Mendeley?

Zotero and Mendeley are the only two reference software managers that are cloud-based and provide the ability for third party application, like PubStream, to access your library content through authentication. Both use the OAuth protocol, which is a widely adopted, open protocol for users to delegate authorization in a secure manner. OAuth is used to authorize requests between PubStream and your reference managers.
PubStream will serve other reference managers as support for OAuth is added.

What makes PubStream and Google Scholar different?

PubStream recommends publications based on the content of your reference library unlike Google Scholar, which makes suggestions based on your publication and citation records only.

How often are recommendations updated?

PubStream updates recommendations daily. You can also subscribe to receive notifications via email on a regular basis.

Which publications sources are fetched by PubStream?

Currently, PubStream fetches publications available on PubMed, the Arxiv.org and articles published in Elsevier journals. That corresponds to about 1,000,000 unique scientific publications per year.
